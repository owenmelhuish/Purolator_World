# Landmark Reference Briefs — image-gen prompts for the case-study worlds

How to use: prepend the **style preamble** to every prompt below, generate,
and bring back the images. One landmark per image. The most useful references
are a single ¾ "hero" view; for anything complex, also ask the generator for
"model sheet: front, side and three-quarter views on one sheet."

## Style preamble (prepend to every prompt)

> Stylized low-poly 3D diorama model, matte flat-shaded materials, soft
> studio lighting, gentle ambient occlusion, clean pastel background,
> isometric three-quarter view, centered single subject on a small circular
> base plate, toy-like miniature architecture aesthetic, crisp geometry with
> minimal bevels, no photorealism, no visible text or lettering (leave blank
> panels where signs would go), 4k render.

Notes for generation:
- **No text in images** — generators mangle type; leave sign faces blank and
  I re-create all lettering/ads in code at full crispness.
- People, if shown, should be simple white minifigure-style characters.
- Keep each brand's hexes in the prompt — colour fidelity matters more than
  detail fidelity.

## World 1 · Choice Hotels Canada (golden-hour travel Canada)

World palette to include in every Choice prompt: warm cream white `#F4EFE3`,
Choice orange `#F57F29`, gold `#FFCE34`, warm umber-brown `#6A634D`.

**1. The Choice Grand Resort (hero, Chapter 1)**
A grand modern Canadian resort hotel on a hilltop plaza: a wide central
six-storey wing flanked by two lower stepped wings, warm white facades with
continuous glass balcony bands, bold orange flat-roof fascia on every wing,
a double-height glass lobby with an orange entrance canopy, a blank white
rooftop sign panel, flagpoles by the entry, and a turquoise outdoor pool
terrace beside it with white loungers, orange umbrellas and a small
poolside family. Golden-hour warmth, resort-postcard feel.

**2. Persona Compass Plaza (Chapter 2)**
A circular travel-planning plaza: a cream stone disc inlaid with a giant
compass rose in orange and tan, a rustic wooden waymarker signpost at the
centre with three blank directional arrow signs at different heights and
angles, and three small traveller vignettes around the rim — a vacation
family with a beach ball, a business traveller pulling a rolling suitcase,
and a winter family wearing little red toques. Space between vignettes so
each reads separately.

**3. Roadside Billboard (creative mile structure)**
A clean modern roadside billboard structure seen at three-quarter view:
wide rounded-rectangle frame in warm off-white, thick blank display panel
(both sides visible), two tapered steel legs, small maintenance catwalk
under the panel, a tiny spotlight bar on top. Elegant, almost furniture-like
proportions — this is the display frame our ad art gets mapped onto.

**4. Choice Privileges Rewards Pavilion (Chapter 4)**
A jewel-like loyalty pavilion: a low golden circular plinth plaza, at its
centre a giant glossy sculpture of a letter "C" built from two interlocking
arcs — a large orange arc and a smaller nested gold arc — standing on a
short stone pedestal like public art; beside it a tall dark monolith shaped
like a smartphone with a blank glowing screen, tilted slightly toward the
viewer; two white minifigures admiring the sculpture. Premium, celebratory.

**5. ROAS 10x+ Proof Monument (Chapter 5)**
A results monument on a rectangular stone terrace: five rounded rectangular
pillars rising left-to-right in a growth staircase (short cream pillars
growing into tall orange and gold ones), behind them a wide dark charcoal
headline slab with a blank face standing on an orange plinth bar. Museum-
of-numbers energy, minimal and confident.

**6. Sub-brand Hotel Row (six separate images if possible)**
Six small distinct roadside hotels sharing one toy-like language, each on
its own pad, each with a blank rooftop sign panel:
- **Comfort** — four-storey navy-blue `#011460` tower, warm yellow-orange
  sunrise accent band `#FFB901`, glass entrance canopy.
- **Quality** — three-storey deep-green `#006A52` inn, cream trim, gold
  awnings.
- **Econo Lodge** — two-storey bright-red `#E31B23` roadside motel with
  outdoor walkways, doors in a row, and a tall retro pole sign (blank).
- **Sleep Inn** — three-storey royal-purple `#4B2884` hotel, pale moon-gold
  crescent accents, calm night-time character.
- **Clarion** — five-storey deep marine navy `#003A59` conference hotel with
  a teal `#0096A7` accent ring and porte-cochère.
- **MainStay Suites** — four-storey slate blue-grey `#294A59` extended-stay
  block with seafoam `#94D4C6` wave accents and residential balconies.

**7. Rockies Ski Lodge (extra)**
An alpine A-frame ski chalet: steep dark-timber A-frame roof to the ground,
full-height glass gable front glowing warm amber from inside, wide wooden
deck with railings, stone chimney, colourful skis racked outside, snow
patches on the roof; two or three jagged low-poly mountains with white snow
caps rising close behind; a few dark-green stylized pines.

**8. Muskoka Cottage Dock (extra)**
Cottage-country lakeside vignette: two small timber cottages with orange
gabled roofs, a long wooden dock running out over flat blue water on post
pilings, a bright red canoe tied alongside, two classic Muskoka chairs on
the shore facing the water, one pine tree. Peaceful summer-evening feel.

**9. Maritime Lighthouse (extra)**
A classic Canadian coastal lighthouse on a rocky islet: tapered tower in
bold red and white horizontal bands, dark gallery ring, glowing amber lamp
room with a red conical cap, standing on a chunky low-poly grey rock island
in the water, one seabird optional.

**10. Trans-Canada Rest Stop (extra)**
A tidy highway rest stop: small café pavilion with a warm-white body,
orange flat canopy roof and one glass wall, a tall blank highway sign on a
post, a timber picnic table, and a parked white-and-orange retro campervan
with an orange side stripe. Road-trip Americana, Canadian edition.

**11. "Experience More" Shuttle Bus (vehicle)**
A friendly modern shuttle coach, toy-proportioned: white body, full-length
orange lower band and thin gold stripe, big windshield, simple black
wheels; side panel left blank for a wrap. Front three-quarter view.

## World 2 · Humber Polytechnic (city campus, relaunch brand)

World palette for every Humber prompt: cool paper white `#EDF0F7`, Humber
navy `#041E42`, gold `#CC9900`, purple accent `#5C068C`, ice-silver blues
`#6E7D9E` to `#BFC6D8`, stage black `#101216`.

**1. Learning Resource Commons — LRC (Chapter 1, campus flagship)**
A six-storey contemporary campus flagship building: full glass curtain-wall
volume gridded by crisp white horizontal floor bands and vertical fins, a
dramatic angled glass entrance atrium jutting from one corner under a navy
canopy, gold accent pylon by the doors, blank navy rooftop sign panel,
students as white minifigures crossing a paved forecourt.

**2. Barrett Centre for Technology Innovation (campus)**
A round high-tech campus drum building: cylindrical glass body wrapped in
evenly spaced vertical navy fins, white ring roof crowned by a floating
thin gold halo ring, a dark blank sign band circling the upper wall.
Landmark-object quality, like a turbine of learning.

**3. Campus Quad (campus)**
A circular campus quad: pale paving with two crossing walking paths, four
slim steel banner poles carrying tall blank black vertical banners, white
minifigure students walking and chatting in small groups, two benches, one
tree. Airy and social.

**4. The Campaign Stage (hero, Chapter 2)**
A theatrical black stage in the open air: a glossy two-tier black circular
stage; at its back a standing sculpture of three stacked, slightly tilted
translucent ice-blue rectangular slabs that catch the light (blank faces);
two tall black lighting masts either side, each with twin theatrical
spotlights aimed down, volumetric white light cones crossing over the
stage; at centre a black grand piano with open lid and a white minifigure
pianist on a bench; two white minifigure students watching from the stage
edge inside pools of light. Dramatic, cinematic, hopeful.

**5. Sankofa Screen Corner (Chapter 3)**
A downtown Toronto-style media corner: two mid-rise buildings meeting at an
angle, each wearing a huge curved blank digital billboard screen glowing
softly (one large, one smaller), cool grey facades with window strips, a
small crowd of white minifigures on the plaza below looking up, one street
lamp. Yonge-Dundas energy, miniature scale.

**6. Lakeshore Heritage Cottage (extra; generate one, I place two)**
A Victorian collegiate heritage building: warm red-brick body with white
stone window surrounds and cornice, steep slate gabled roof, a small square
central tower with a pyramidal cap, arched entrance door, sitting on a neat
lawn near water. Dignified, 1880s institutional charm.

**7. Hawks Athletics Field (extra)**
A compact college sports field: bright green pitch with white line markings,
two low navy grandstands with gold trim facing each other, four corner
floodlight masts with glowing heads, a navy scoreboard on a post with a
blank display face. Game-day miniature.

**8. Live Campaign Room — Data Pavilion (Chapter 4)**
An open-front glass pavilion on a circular pad: flat purple roof slab on
four purple corner columns, glass side walls, no front wall; inside, a
large freestanding dark dashboard wall with a blank glowing screen face,
two white minifigure analysts standing before it. Mission-control calm.

**9. +3% Impact Monument (Chapter 5)**
A results monument: wide dark charcoal slab with a blank face on a purple
plinth bar, and beside it a bold gold arrow sculpture that rises in three
angled segments from the ground and points steeply upward, arrowhead
soaring past the slab's top corner. Momentum made physical.

**10. Campaign Streetcar (vehicle)**
A modern articulated city streetcar, toy proportions: two segments joined
by a dark bellows, light-grey body with a red skirt band and nose, long
window band, small pantograph on the roof, sides left blank for a black
campaign wrap. Side three-quarter view.

## World 3 · CIRA (Canadian-internet Canadiana, Maple Leaf Red)

World palette for every CIRA prompt: paper cream `#F5F1E8`, Maple Leaf Red
`#AA1E3A` (bright accent `#BA2241`, dark `#7E0E27`), navy `#0C1C2B`,
prairie gold `#DEDAB7`/`#9E8E56`, maple-leaf fall reds and oranges, and the
red-and-black buffalo plaid pattern.

**1. The .CA Monument (hero, Chapter 1)**
A monumental typographic sculpture on a cream stone terrace: giant
three-dimensional letters "CA" in glossy Maple Leaf Red with a large
separate glossy red sphere sitting at ground level before them as the dot
of ".CA", letters thick and architectural like public art you can walk
around; two white minifigures for scale.

**2. CIRA Headquarters (Chapter 1, beside the monument)**
A dignified Ottawa institutional building in miniature: warm red-brick
facade with cream stone corner quoins and cornice, regular window grid, a
green oxidized-copper mansard roof, formal stone entrance, and a blank
rooftop sign panel. Parliamentary-precinct flavour, toy scale.

**3. Bernard the Goose Plaza (hero, Chapter 2)**
A circular plaza whose entire floor is red-and-black buffalo plaid, with a
gold-trimmed dark-red circular plinth at centre carrying a proud, slightly
menacing Canada goose statue mid-honk — grey-brown body, cream belly,
long black neck extended forward, black head with white chinstrap patch,
open black bill; a small angled dark plaque stand before it (blank), a tiny
pizza-shop kiosk with a red awning at the plaza edge, and one nervous white
minifigure shop-owner looking up at the goose.

**4. Canadian Shield Dome (Chapter 4)**
A protective dome over a neighbourhood: four tiny white houses with
maritime-blue roofs and one white minifigure family inside a large
translucent rose-red glass dome with a bright red rim ring at its base;
outside the dome, three small dark angular shards frozen mid-bounce as they
deflect off the surface. Guardian-shield sci-fi warmth, still friendly.

**5. .CA Registry Data Centre (extra)**
A sleek registry building: long low white hall with a bold red roof band,
its front wall open to reveal a wall of dark navy server racks with tiny
glowing red and blue status lights, and a large blank ticker display panel
mounted above the roofline on posts. Precision infrastructure, warm not
cold.

**6. IXP Exchange Node (extra; one design, I place four)**
A small internet exchange station: compact navy equipment cabin on a round
cream pad, topped by a slender mast carrying a glowing red-pink energy orb,
a small blank label plaque on its face. Looks like a friendly network
totem; the glowing orb is where red data arcs land.

**7. Net Good Northern Community (extra)**
A northern community vignette on a snowy pad: four small timber cabins with
white snow-capped roofs and warm amber windows, a tall steel communications
mast with two crossbars and a glowing red beacon at its tip, soft snow
drifts; above and behind, a hint of green-teal aurora curtain in the sky.
Warm lights in the north.

**8. +66% Recall Monument (Chapter 5)**
A results monument: wide slab with a blank face finished in a deep red
gradient (maple red fading to near-black maroon) on a red plinth bar,
standing on a cream terrace; three small Canada geese standing on the
terrace around it like an honour guard.

**9. Bernard (creature model, standing + flying poses)**
A stylized low-poly Canada goose in two poses on one sheet: standing
(upright S-curve neck, grey-brown body, cream belly, black tail, black
neck and head with white chinstrap, black bill and legs) and flying (wings
spread wide, neck extended straight forward, legs tucked). Toy-figurine
quality, slightly characterful and self-important.

**10. ".CA by cira" Delivery Van (vehicle)**
A compact panel van, toy proportions: Maple Leaf Red body, white roof,
simple black wheels, side panel left blank for the .ca lockup. Front
three-quarter view.
